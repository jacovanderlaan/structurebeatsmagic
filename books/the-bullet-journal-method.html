The Bullet Journal Method

Ryder Carroll · 2018 · pkm notes

Highlights

  • Rapid logging: typed capture with minimal syntax — Carroll's bullets encode type at capture time (task, event, note, with signifiers), the analog ancestor of a capture-type convention like transaction_/idea_/decision_ prefixes: classify on the way in, cheaply.
  • Migration is curation with built-in friction — periodically re-writing open items by hand forces the question of whether each one still deserves to exist; deliberate friction as a review rule, something frictionless digital capture quietly loses and must re-add.
  • The index makes a linear notebook navigable — pages stay chronological while the index provides addressable access; the insight that a thin metadata layer beats reorganizing content is the same one behind frontmatter-driven vault navigation.
  • Daily, monthly, and future logs form a time spine — the notebook's backbone is the calendar itself, with content hanging off dates; calendar-as-spine (PDR-065) is this exact architecture at vault scale.
  • Collections coexist with the time axis — topic pages live alongside dated logs, reconciling chronological capture with thematic organization; the day-folder-plus-domain-folder duality in notebook form.
  • The system exists for intentionality, not throughput — Carroll frames the method as a reflection practice that regularly asks why an item matters, a reminder that a second brain measured only by capture volume has missed its own point.
