Highlights (AI · MDDE/SBM lens)

AI-distilled highlights, not personal reading highlights — the ideas in this book that connect to Jaco's MDDE / Structure-Beats-Magic work, selected and paraphrased through that lens. Paraphrased (never verbatim); verbatim quotes await the book text.

  • A latticework of named models is a concept library — Weinberg and McCann's anthology of hundreds of named, cross-referenced thinking tools is structurally the same artifact as a folder-per-concept library: naming plus curation makes ideas addressable and reusable.
  • Naming a pattern makes it deployable — once "5 Whys" or "Eisenhower matrix" has a handle, you can invoke it under pressure; the same reason concepts in a knowledge graph need canonical names before they can link articles together.
  • Forcing functions beat willpower — the book's advice to engineer situations where the right behavior happens by default is structure-beats-magic stated as a mental model: build the mechanism, don't rely on discipline.
  • The wrong model is worse than no model — applying a mental model outside its domain produces confident nonsense, which argues for curation and fit over sheer collection size; a lesson for both concept libraries and architecture pattern catalogs.
  • Inversion and first principles as analysis rules — working backwards from failure and rebuilding from fundamentals are repeatable procedures, not talents; codifiable moves that belong in any decision-record practice.
  • Models compound across domains — the payoff of the latticework comes from combinations (feedback loops + incentives + inertia), mirroring how a connected note vault outperforms isolated notes: value lives in the edges.
