Visual Doing

Willemien Brand · 2019 · design

Highlights

  • Drawing is a learnable system, not a talent — Brand's core promise is that anyone can communicate visually by mastering a small set of teachable building blocks, not by waiting for artistic gift. That is structure beats magic in miniature: what looks like creative "magic" decomposes into rules and skills you can practice deliberately.
  • A shared visual vocabulary beats improvised sketches — the book builds on a compact library of reusable elements (simple figures, icons, arrows, frames) that combine into endless business stories. This is a concept-library move: agree on a precise shared vocabulary once, then compose from it, instead of reinventing notation in every meeting.
  • Making thinking visible exposes misunderstanding early — when you draw an idea live in front of colleagues, gaps and disagreements surface immediately because everyone reacts to the same concrete picture. That is validate-don't-guess: an externalized model, however rough, gets tested against reality far faster than words floating in a discussion.
  • The follow-up to Visual Thinking is about doing, in daily work — where the first book made the case, this one applies visual communication to everyday situations: meetings, presentations, team sessions, change stories. Same pattern as MDDE: the value is not in admiring the method but in embedding it into the routine workflow where the work actually happens.
  • Simple beats polished — Brand consistently favors quick, rough, functional drawings over designed perfection, because the goal is shared understanding, not decoration. That mirrors business-friendly modeling: a diagram a stakeholder can read and correct outperforms a technically flawless artifact nobody engages with.
  • Pen and paper outlast every tool — the capability the book teaches works on a whiteboard, a napkin, or a tablet; the medium is interchangeable. Platforms churn, capabilities endure: invest in the visual-communication skill and the underlying vocabulary, not in any particular drawing app.
