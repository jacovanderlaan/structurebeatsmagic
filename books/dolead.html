Do/Lead

Les McKeown · 2014 · business

Highlights

  • Leadership is a practice, not a personality — McKeown's core move is stripping leadership of its mystique: it is a set of learnable, repeatable behaviours available to anyone, not a charisma gene. This is structure-beats-magic applied to people: replace the "born leader" myth with an explicit, teachable pattern.
  • Debunk the hero myth before it costs you — the book argues that waiting for (or worshipping) a visionary saviour makes organisations fragile, because everything hinges on one irreplaceable person. Same failure mode as hand-crafted data pipelines: anything that depends on a single magician instead of a shared system does not survive that person leaving.
  • Lead from where you are — leadership in McKeown's telling happens in small, local acts embedded in daily work, not in grand gestures from the top. That is the atomic-units principle for behaviour: many small, self-contained acts of leading compound into organisational capability, the way atomic notes compound into a second brain.
  • The role outlives the person — because leadership is defined as behaviours rather than a title, it can be handed over, distributed, and picked up by others without loss. The model outlives the tool: encode what leading means in shared, explicit terms and any individual becomes replaceable without the capability collapsing.
  • Self-awareness as a feedback loop, not a trait — McKeown treats knowing your own leadership style and blind spots as ongoing observation of your actual behaviour, not a one-time personality label. That is validate-don't-guess turned inward: check what you really do against what you believe you do, and correct on the evidence.
  • Groups need many leaders, which needs shared language — the book's distributed-leadership stance only works when everyone means the same thing by "leading" in a given moment. That is a concept-library problem: a team that shares precise vocabulary for its leadership behaviours can rotate the role fluidly; one that doesn't defaults back to waiting for the hero.
