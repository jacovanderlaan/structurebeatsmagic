Highlights

  • Empowered teams solve problems; feature teams ship output — Cagan's central distinction: give a team an outcome to achieve rather than a backlog to burn down; organizational structure determining product quality is structure-beats-magic at the org-chart level.
  • Discovery is systematic risk retirement — test the four risks (value, usability, feasibility, business viability) cheaply before building; the product-world twin of resolving ambiguity at requirements time instead of paying for it in production.
  • Prototypes over documents for learning — a cheap testable artifact falsifies assumptions faster than any specification review; evidence-generating structure beating persuasive prose.
  • The best companies work structurally differently — Cagan's point about Amazon, Netflix, and Google is not that they have better people or better process theater, but a fundamentally different operating structure; adopting agile ceremonies without that structure is cargo-culting the magic.
  • Product management is a knowledge role — deep customer knowledge, data knowledge, and business knowledge, not backlog administration; competence as accumulated, structured understanding rather than facilitation technique.
  • Dual-track: continuous discovery feeding continuous delivery — two parallel structured loops rather than a phase gate; cadenced learning wired into the delivery system the way review cadences are wired into a trusted personal system.
