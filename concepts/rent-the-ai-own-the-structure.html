Rent the AI; Own the Structure

Models are swappable commodities; your structure is the durable asset — so you're never locked to one vendor's AI.

Models are swappable commodities; your structure is the durable asset — so you're never locked to one vendor's AI.

Models come and go and are rented from vendors; the structure you build — content, structure, rules, skills — is the durable asset you own. So invest there, not in the model.

The sharper claim (portability): if your context, rules and skills live as files beside the model rather than baked into one vendor's ecosystem, you can swap the model underneath without rebuilding the system. The model doesn't recall the answer from memory; you hand it the structure and it reasons over that. Change the engine, keep the car.

The proof: run the same structure + rules through two different models — e.g. Claude and the European Mistral — and the system keeps working. That's not a thought experiment; it's testable, and it doubles as evidence for [[concept-structure-beats-magic|Structure Beats Magic]] (the good output was in the structure, not the model).

The sovereignty dimension: model-portability is the AI-layer of data sovereignty ([[data-soevereiniteit-en-homelab-richting]]) — you're not tied to a US hyperscaler, you can run an EU model (Mistral) with data in Europe, and you can switch on a ToS change, price rise or compliance requirement without losing your system. Especially load-bearing for the banking/governance audience (BCBS 239, GDPR).
